🌿 GBIF Record Finder

A free, browser-based tool that lets ecologists, conservation planners, and environmental consultants find biodiversity records from GBIF for any user-defined polygon — with no login, no record limits, and no software to install.

How it works

  • Draw a polygon on the map or upload a KML/KMZ file
  • Select taxon groups and date range
  • The tool submits a download request to the GBIF occurrence API and polls until ready
  • Records are parsed, mapped, and exported — all in your browser

Who it's for

Ecologists, EIA consultants, conservation planners, citizen scientists, and anyone who needs a quick, clean spatial extract of GBIF data without navigating the main portal.

Data & citation

All data is sourced from GBIF. Please cite GBIF when publishing results based on this tool. Use the format: GBIF.org (year), GBIF Occurrence Download, https://doi.org/[your download DOI]

Data disclaimer

  • Records reflect what has been submitted to GBIF — not a comprehensive survey.
  • Rare and threatened species are often obscured or absent.
  • Individual records may contain errors in identification, location, or date.
  • Use as a starting point only — not authoritative data for regulatory purposes.
