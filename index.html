🌿 GBIF Occurrence Extractor

A free, browser-based tool that lets ecologists, conservation planners, and environmental consultants extract biodiversity occurrence data from GBIF for any user-defined polygon — with no login, no record limits, and no software to install.

How it works

  • Draw a polygon on the map or upload a KML/KMZ file
  • Select taxon groups and date range
  • The tool submits a download request to the GBIF occurrence API and polls until ready
  • Records are parsed, mapped, and exported — all in your browser

Who it's for

Ecologists, EIA consultants, conservation planners, citizen scientists, and anyone who needs a quick, clean spatial extract of GBIF data without navigating the main portal.

Data & citation

All data is sourced from GBIF. Every download generates a citable DOI — shown after each fetch. Please cite GBIF when publishing results based on this tool.

Data disclaimer

This tool provides access to biodiversity occurrence data sourced from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF). Users should be aware of the following limitations:

  • Data reflects what has been recorded and submitted to GBIF, and is not a comprehensive survey of any area.
  • Location data for some species may be absent or inaccurate where records have been obscured prior to submission to GBIF. This obscuring is biased towards rare and threatened species, meaning the tool may underrepresent precisely those species of greatest conservation interest.
  • Individual records may contain errors in identification, location, or date.
  • This tool is intended as a starting point for investigation, not as authoritative data for regulatory or legal purposes.
